Understanding the Basics

Roulette is one of the most iconic casino games worldwide, known for its spinning wheel and the thrill of predicting where the ball will land. Two popular variations dominate the casino world: American Roulette and European Roulette.

American Roulette: Double the Excitement

American Roulette features a wheel with 38 pockets: numbers 1 to 36, plus a single zero (0) and a double zero (00). The additional double zero increases the house edge, making it slightly more challenging for players. However, it also adds to the excitement and potential for larger payouts.

European Roulette: Better Odds for Players

European Roulette, on the other hand, comes with just 37 pockets—numbers 1 to 36 and a single zero (0). This minor difference significantly reduces the house edge, offering players a better chance of winning. Many seasoned players prefer European Roulette for its improved odds.

Key Differences Between American and European Roulette

  • House Edge: American Roulette: 5.26% vs. European Roulette: 2.7%
  • Wheel Layout: American has 38 slots; European has 37 slots.
  • Double Zero: Only American Roulette includes the double zero (00).
  • Gameplay: European Roulette often offers the "En Prison" and "La Partage" rules for better odds.

Which One Should You Play?

The choice depends on your goals. If you prefer better odds and more strategic play, European Roulette is the way to go. For those seeking more thrill and higher payouts, American Roulette can be more rewarding. You can try both at Teen Patti Stars to find your perfect match.
